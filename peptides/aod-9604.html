About AOD-9604

AOD-9604 is a synthetic 16-amino acid modified fragment of the C-terminus of human growth hormone (residues 176–191), with an additional disulfide bond for stability. It is studied in research for its effects on lipolysis and adipose tissue regulation in animal models.

AOD-9604 is sold worldwide as a research-use-only (RUO) compound. Purity and consistency vary between suppliers, which is why third-party lab testing (HPLC, mass spectrometry) and certificates of analysis matter when comparing options.

AOD-9604 vs HGH Fragment 176-191

AOD-9604 and HGH Fragment 176-191 share the same core 16-amino acid sequence but differ in stability:

  • Disulfide bond — AOD-9604 includes an extra disulfide bond not present in unmodified HGH Frag 176-191
  • Stability — the modification was designed to extend stability and shelf-life in research samples
  • Sequence — both are derived from residues 176–191 of the C-terminus of hGH
  • Research history — AOD-9604 was investigated as an obesity therapeutic candidate but did not advance clinically; both remain RUO research peptides
