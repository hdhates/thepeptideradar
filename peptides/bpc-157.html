BPC-157 vs TB-500 vs GHK-Cu

Three peptides commonly studied in tissue repair, wound healing, and recovery research — with distinct mechanisms:

  • Origin — BPC-157 is synthetic, derived from a gastric protective protein; TB-500 is a synthetic version of Thymosin Beta-4, a naturally occurring peptide; GHK-Cu is a naturally occurring copper-binding tripeptide
  • Primary mechanism — BPC-157: gut lining protection, tendon/ligament repair, systemic tissue healing; TB-500: actin polymerization, angiogenesis, wound closure; GHK-Cu: skin remodeling, collagen synthesis, antioxidant signaling
  • Research depth — BPC-157 has the broadest preclinical literature of the three across multiple tissue types; TB-500 is heavily studied for musculoskeletal endpoints; GHK-Cu has strong in vitro data for skin and hair
  • Common comparison — BPC-157 and TB-500 are frequently discussed together in musculoskeletal repair research; GHK-Cu is more distinct, studied primarily in dermatological and hair growth contexts

What is BPC-157?

BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound 157) is a synthetic peptide derived from a protective protein found in human gastric juice. It is widely studied in research for its effects on tissue repair, gut health, and tendon healing in animal models.
