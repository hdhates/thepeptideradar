About Hexarelin

Hexarelin is a synthetic hexapeptide GHSR agonist and one of the most potent GH-releasing peptides studied in research. It is structurally related to GHRP-6 but produces a stronger GH pulse at comparable doses. Like GHRP-6, it elevates cortisol and prolactin more than selective GHRPs such as Ipamorelin.

Hexarelin has also been studied for cardiovascular endpoints through GHS-R1a receptors expressed in cardiac tissue — a research application distinct from its GH-releasing activity. It is sold worldwide as a research-use-only (RUO) compound with purity verification via third-party HPLC and mass spectrometry.

Hexarelin vs GHRP-6 vs Ipamorelin

Three GHSR agonists studied in GH-axis and cardiovascular research — compared by potency, selectivity, and mechanism:

  • GH release potency — Hexarelin produces the strongest GH pulse of the three; GHRP-6 is moderate; Ipamorelin produces a cleaner pulse with less amplitude but greater selectivity
  • Cortisol & prolactin — Hexarelin raises both substantially; GHRP-6 moderate; Ipamorelin minimal — making Ipamorelin the preferred option when GH-only research is the goal
  • Cardiovascular research — Hexarelin uniquely has preclinical data on cardioprotective effects via cardiac GHS-R1a receptors, independent of GH release; the other two do not have this specific research profile
  • Half-life — Hexarelin ~2 hours; GHRP-6 ~15–60 minutes; Ipamorelin ~2 hours

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Hexarelin?

Hexarelin is a synthetic hexapeptide GHSR agonist and one of the most potent GH-releasing peptides studied in research. It elevates cortisol and prolactin more than selective GHRPs like Ipamorelin but has also been studied for cardiovascular endpoints independent of the GH axis.

How is Hexarelin different from GHRP-6?

Hexarelin generally produces a stronger GH pulse than GHRP-6 but also raises cortisol and prolactin more. GHRP-6 has a more pronounced appetite-stimulating ghrelin effect. Both are less selective than Ipamorelin for GH-only research.

Does Hexarelin have cardiovascular research applications?

Yes. Hexarelin has been studied for cardioprotective effects in preclinical research through GHS-R1a receptors expressed in cardiac tissue — separate from its GH-releasing activity. This is a distinct research application compared to other GHRPs.
