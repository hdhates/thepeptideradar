About Semax

Semax is a synthetic heptapeptide (Met-Glu-His-Phe-Pro-Gly-Pro) derived from the ACTH(4-10) fragment. Developed in Russia, it has been studied extensively for neuroprotective and cognitive endpoints, including upregulation of brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) and neuroplasticity research. An acetylated variant, N-Acetyl Semax, offers enhanced potency and longer action.

Semax is sold worldwide as a research-use-only (RUO) compound. It is often compared to Selank for nootropic research. Purity and formulation vary significantly between suppliers — third-party lab data is the key signal when comparing options.

Semax vs N-Acetyl Semax vs Selank

Three closely related nootropic peptides from the Russian research tradition — each with distinct properties:

  • Origin — Semax and N-Acetyl Semax derive from the ACTH(4-10) fragment; Selank derives from tuftsin — a different peptide class entirely
  • Effect profile — Semax is studied for stimulating, cognitive-enhancing effects with BDNF upregulation; Selank has more anxiolytic and calming properties; N-Acetyl Semax is generally considered more potent than base Semax
  • Duration — N-Acetyl Semax has a longer effective duration than unmodified Semax due to enhanced membrane permeability; Selank has a comparable short half-life to base Semax
  • Research pairing — Semax and Selank are sometimes studied together for complementary cognitive and anxiolytic endpoints

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Semax?

Semax is a synthetic heptapeptide (Met-Glu-His-Phe-Pro-Gly-Pro) derived from the ACTH(4-10) fragment. It is studied in research for neuroprotective and cognitive endpoints, including BDNF upregulation and neuroplasticity.

What is the difference between Semax and N-Acetyl Semax?

N-Acetyl Semax is an acetylated analog of Semax with enhanced membrane permeability and a longer effective duration. Both are studied for similar cognitive and neuroprotective endpoints, but N-Acetyl Semax is generally considered more potent per unit dose.

How is Semax different from Selank?

Both are nootropic peptides with Russian research origins. Semax (ACTH-derived) tends to produce more stimulating, cognitive-enhancing effects with BDNF upregulation. Selank (tuftsin-derived) has more pronounced anxiolytic and calming properties. They are often studied together for complementary effects.
