About Selank

Selank is a synthetic heptapeptide developed in Russia, structurally based on the natural immunomodulatory peptide tuftsin. In research it is studied for anxiolytic-like effects, immune modulation, and cognitive endpoints in animal models.

Selank is sold worldwide as a research-use-only (RUO) compound, often distributed in nasal-spray or lyophilized form. Purity and consistency vary between suppliers, which is why third-party lab testing (HPLC, mass spectrometry) and certificates of analysis matter when comparing options.

Selank vs Semax

Both Selank and Semax are Russian-developed synthetic peptides commonly studied for nootropic and neuropeptide-like research effects. Key distinctions in the literature:

  • Selank — most often associated with anxiolytic-like research endpoints and immune modulation
  • Semax — typically associated with cognitive and neurotrophic research endpoints; an acetylated variant (N-Acetyl Semax) offers enhanced potency
  • Form factor — both commonly distributed in nasal-spray or lyophilized form for research use
  • Storage — research lyophilized peptides are typically refrigerated; reconstituted forms have a shorter shelf life
