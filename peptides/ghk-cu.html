About GHK-Cu

GHK-Cu is a naturally occurring copper-binding tripeptide (glycyl-histidyl-lysyl copper) — sometimes simply called "copper peptide." It is widely studied in research for skin remodeling, wound healing, hair growth signaling, and antioxidant effects.

GHK-Cu is sold worldwide as a research-use-only compound. Because copper-binding stability matters, third-party HPLC and mass spectrometry testing is critical for purity verification.

GHK-Cu vs BPC-157 vs Epithalon

Three peptides studied in skin, cellular health, and regenerative research — each with a distinct mechanism:

  • Origin — GHK-Cu is a naturally occurring copper-binding tripeptide; BPC-157 is a synthetic 15-amino acid gastric-derived peptide; Epithalon is a synthetic tetrapeptide developed in Russia
  • Primary mechanism — GHK-Cu: copper-mediated collagen synthesis, antioxidant signaling, wound healing; BPC-157: systemic tissue repair, gut lining, tendon healing; Epithalon: telomere regulation, melatonin-related signaling, studied for longevity endpoints
  • Research context — GHK-Cu has strong in vitro and cosmetic research; BPC-157 has broad preclinical data across tissue types; Epithalon has primarily Russian-origin research focused on aging and telomere biology
  • Sourcing note — GHK-Cu is widely available; BPC-157 has the most suppliers; Epithalon is stocked by fewer specialty sources
