About GHRP-6

GHRP-6 (Growth Hormone Releasing Peptide-6) is a synthetic hexapeptide and GHSR agonist. It is one of the earliest GH secretagogues studied in research and has served as a benchmark compound for comparing newer GHRPs. Unlike more selective peptides like Ipamorelin, GHRP-6 significantly raises ghrelin — producing notable appetite stimulation alongside GH release — and can elevate cortisol and prolactin.

GHRP-6 is sold worldwide as a research-use-only (RUO) compound. It is sold as a lyophilized powder and is often compared to GHRP-2 and Ipamorelin in GH-axis research. Third-party HPLC and mass spectrometry COAs remain the key signal when comparing suppliers.

GHRP-6 vs GHRP-2 vs Ipamorelin

Three classic GHSR agonists studied in GH-axis research — with distinct selectivity and side-effect profiles:

  • GH release potency — GHRP-2 generally produces the strongest GH pulse; GHRP-6 is moderate; Ipamorelin produces a cleaner but slightly smaller pulse
  • Ghrelin / appetite — GHRP-6 raises ghrelin significantly, causing notable hunger; GHRP-2 and Ipamorelin have less appetite effect; this is the defining characteristic of GHRP-6 research
  • Cortisol & prolactin — GHRP-2 raises both the most; GHRP-6 moderate elevation; Ipamorelin has minimal cortisol or prolactin effect — the key reason it is considered more selective
  • Half-life — GHRP-6 and GHRP-2 ~15–60 minutes; Ipamorelin ~2 hours

Frequently Asked Questions

What is GHRP-6?

GHRP-6 (Growth Hormone Releasing Peptide-6) is a synthetic hexapeptide GHSR agonist. It is studied in research for growth hormone release, though it also raises ghrelin (causing appetite stimulation) and elevates cortisol and prolactin — making it less selective than newer GHRPs like Ipamorelin.

What is the difference between GHRP-6 and GHRP-2?

Both are hexapeptide GHSR agonists. GHRP-2 generally produces a stronger GH pulse but also elevates prolactin and cortisol more. GHRP-6 has a stronger appetite-stimulating effect through ghrelin. Both are considered less selective than Ipamorelin for GH-only research.

How is GHRP-6 different from Ipamorelin?

Ipamorelin is a pentapeptide with high selectivity — it stimulates GH release with minimal cortisol, prolactin, or ghrelin elevation. GHRP-6 is less selective and significantly raises ghrelin (causing hunger) and can spike cortisol. Ipamorelin has a longer half-life (~2 hours vs ~15–60 minutes for GHRP-6).
