About TB-500

TB-500 is a synthetic version of Thymosin Beta-4 (TB-4), a naturally occurring peptide found throughout the body. It is widely studied in research for its effects on tissue repair, recovery, and inflammation in animal models.

TB-500 is sold worldwide as a research-use-only (RUO) compound. Purity and consistency vary between suppliers, which is why third-party lab testing (HPLC, mass spectrometry) and certificates of analysis matter.

TB-500 vs BPC-157 vs Thymosin Alpha-1

Three peptides that carry the "Thymosin" name or are studied alongside it — with largely non-overlapping mechanisms:

  • Naming — TB-500 is a synthetic version of Thymosin Beta-4. Thymosin Alpha-1 is an entirely different peptide from the thymus gland despite sharing the "Thymosin" name. BPC-157 is unrelated — it's a gastric-derived synthetic peptide.
  • Mechanism — TB-500: actin sequestration, angiogenesis, tissue repair; Thymosin Alpha-1: T-cell and dendritic cell immune modulation; BPC-157: gut-protective, systemic tissue and tendon healing
  • Research focus — TB-500 and BPC-157 are both studied for musculoskeletal and systemic repair; Thymosin Alpha-1 is studied for immune defense and has approved clinical use in several countries (Zadaxin)
  • Supplier availability — BPC-157 has the widest supplier availability of the three; TB-500 and Thymosin Alpha-1 are stocked by fewer specialists
