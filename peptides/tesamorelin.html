About Tesamorelin

Tesamorelin is a synthetic 44-amino acid analog of growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH), with a trans-3-hexenoic acid modification at the N-terminus that extends its plasma stability. It is studied in research for its effects on endogenous growth hormone secretion and visceral adipose tissue.

Tesamorelin is sold worldwide as a research-use-only (RUO) compound. Purity and consistency vary between suppliers, which is why third-party lab testing (HPLC, mass spectrometry) and certificates of analysis matter when comparing options.

Tesamorelin vs Sermorelin vs CJC-1295

All three are GHRH analogs studied in research, but with distinct pharmacokinetic profiles:

  • Sequence length — Sermorelin is a 29-aa fragment; Tesamorelin is the full 44-aa GHRH sequence; CJC-1295 is a 30-aa modified analog
  • Half-life — Sermorelin ~10 minutes; Tesamorelin ~25–40 minutes; CJC-1295 with DAC several days
  • Modification — Tesamorelin's N-terminal trans-3-hexenoic acid resists DPP-IV cleavage, extending stability over Sermorelin
  • Research dosing — protocols differ significantly between the three due to half-life differences
