About Sermorelin

Sermorelin is a synthetic 29-amino acid peptide and a GHRH (growth hormone-releasing hormone) analog. In research it is studied for its effect on stimulating endogenous growth hormone secretion via the pituitary gland.

Sermorelin is sold worldwide as a research-use-only (RUO) compound. Purity and consistency vary between suppliers, which is why third-party lab testing (HPLC, mass spectrometry) and certificates of analysis matter when comparing options.

Sermorelin vs CJC-1295

Both Sermorelin and CJC-1295 are GHRH analogs studied for their effect on the pituitary gland. The key research-pharmacokinetic distinctions:

  • Half-life — Sermorelin has a short half-life (~10 minutes); CJC-1295 with DAC extends to several days due to drug affinity complex
  • Sequence length — Sermorelin is 29 amino acids; CJC-1295 is a modified 30-amino acid analog
  • Research dosing — protocols differ significantly between the two due to the half-life difference
  • Stack pairing — both are commonly studied alongside Ipamorelin for GH pulse amplification; see also the CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin guide
